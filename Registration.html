Frequently Asked Questions

1) When and where will the Fuzzing and Software Security Summer School 2025 take place?

The Summer School will be hosted by the School of Computing at the National University of Singapore.  The school will be held over the week of the 26th-30th May 2025.

2) Who is eligible to participate in the summer school?

The school is open to students, postdocs, faculty, and industry practitioners who are interested in software security and fuzz testing.  Both local and international applications are welcome.  Some background in software security or software testing will aid your application. Generally the audience is mixed. 

3) What is the focus of the summer school?

The general focus of the summer school is software testing (fuzzing) and security.  The school will feature instruction from five International experts in the area of fuzz testing, both from academia and industry. Students are expected to gain insights and knowledge in the latest trends and technologies related to fuzzing and software security in general. Hands-on exercises will be provided in this edition of the summer school.

4) Can industry professionals attend the summer school?

Yes, the school is open to both current students and industry professionals.

5) How can I register for the Fuzzing and Software Security Summer School?

The registration portal is expected to open in Mid-Jan 2025.  If there are any technical problems, please contact fuzzing@comp.nus.edu.sg.

6) What are the registration fees for the summer school?

Please visit the registration page for the fee schedule.  Discounts are available for local (Singaporean) students and government agencies.

7) Is accommodation provided as part of the registration fee?

Accommodation is not provided as part of the package.Please also note that the Fuzzing Summer School organizers will not be involved in arranging accommodation for participants. Visa letters may be requested by selected attendees at fuzzing@comp.nus.edu.sg  The organizers will not be able to help with the visa applications in any way, beyond providing the letter. Attendees may email us on this, only after they are selected for the summer school.

9) What does the registration fee cover?

The fee covers the entire 5 day programme, and includes lunch, tea breaks, social events and a banquet dinner.

10) Is there any financial support or scholarships available for participants?

At this point we do not have such arrangements. 

11) Can I get insights into the networking opportunities at the summer school?

G enerally we expect a good mix of attendees, including both students and industry professionals. The speakers of the summer school as well as the organizing team are established researchers in the field with a high research footprint. Therefore, attending the summer school can be very beneficial for networking and future research connections, apart from knowledge in software security.
