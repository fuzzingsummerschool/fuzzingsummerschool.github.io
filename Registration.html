Frequently Asked Questions

1) When and where will the Fuzzing and Software Security Summer School 2026 take place?

The Summer School will be hosted by the School of Computing at the National University of Singapore. The school will be held from 25 to 29 May 2026.

2) Who is eligible to participate in the summer school?

The school is open to students, postdocs, faculty, and industry practitioners who are interested in software security and fuzz testing. Both local and international applications are welcome. Some background in software security or software testing will aid your application.

3) What is the focus of the summer school?

The general focus is software testing (fuzzing) and security. The school will feature instruction from five international experts from both academia and industry. Students will gain insights into the latest trends and technologies, with hands-on exercises provided.

4) Can industry professionals attend the summer school?

Yes, the school is open to both current students and industry professionals.

5) How can I register for the Fuzzing and Software Security Summer School?

The registration portal is expected to open in Mid-Jan 2025. If there are any technical problems, please contact fuzzing@nus.edu.sg.

6) What are the registration fees for the summer school?

Please refer to the fees table above for the schedule. Discounts are available for local (Singaporean) students and government agencies.

7) Is accommodation provided as part of the registration fee?

Accommodation is not provided. Participants are responsible for arranging their own stay. The organizers will not be involved in arranging accommodation but may provide general information for the benefit of attendees.
